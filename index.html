What is FIA?

The Feminine Intelligence Agency (FIA) methodology is a structured analytical framework for identifying personality types and behavioral patterns in characters. It reads behavior through 28 bipolar trait dimensions and matches characters to one of 17 player types — ranging from healthy to predatory.

The methodology rests on one core principle: catch deterministically, interpret separately, hold cause open. Every finding traces back to specific utterances from the script. Nothing is asserted without evidence.

Key Rule: Score the function of behavior, not its affect. A calm, composed character is not automatically healthy — the question is what their composure accomplishes in the interaction.

Who is Edward?

Edward Cullen is best understood not as an overt predator and not as a conventionally healthy romantic partner. He is a highly controlled, hyper-independent character whose primary relational strategy is managing uncertainty through information control.

His strongest recurring pattern is not overt aggression but narrative dominance: Bella repeatedly perceives reality accurately, Edward reframes or withholds, and only later confirms what she already knew.

Key Finding: Edward's calm demeanor is not evidence of health. Once behavior is scored by function rather than affect, scenes that initially read as protective instead read as exercises of informational control over the relationship.

Who is Bella?

Bella Swan is a Dreamer — a target-side personality whose core disposition is deep trust, romantic idealization, and a capacity for total emotional investment. The same trait that makes her open and genuine is the one that creates specific vulnerability to the patterns Edward exhibits.

The Dreamer's strength is authentic emotional depth. Her exposure comes from the same source: she extends trust and meaning ahead of evidence, which leaves her vulnerable to partners who can mirror her investment without reciprocating it genuinely.

Key Finding: Bella is frequently right. She correctly perceives Edward's concealment, his incomplete explanations, and the supernatural before he confirms any of it. Her self-doubt is not evidence of irrationality — it is evidence of the interaction's effect on her perception.

Bella's Vulnerability Profile

Strength

Deep emotional authenticity. Bella's trust is real and her investment is genuine. She does not perform connection — she lives it. This is what makes her compelling as a character and as a person.

Exposure

Because she extends trust ahead of evidence, a partner who can simulate depth and investment without reciprocating it genuinely can hold her attention indefinitely through intermittent reinforcement.

Danger Signature

Bella's fear arc runs from curiosity to fear-tested calm to deepening attachment — a trajectory of rising trust against stated danger rather than rising fear. She is not frightened where she should be.

Cognitive Pattern

Romantic idealization causes her to interpret Edward's withholding as mystery rather than control. His danger declarations become evidence of his special nature rather than warnings.

The Lock and Key

FIA's pairing analysis asks: how does this specific player type fit with this specific target personality? Not every predator-target combination is equally dangerous. Some combinations are structurally misaligned. The Edward-Bella pairing is structurally aligned in ways that matter.

Core Dynamic: Edward's primary control mechanism is informational asymmetry and intermittent reinforcement. Bella's primary vulnerability is trust extended ahead of evidence and romantic idealization. He controls information; she fills the gaps with meaning. The lock fits the key.

How the Patterns Interact

Edward's Move

Withholds information about his nature, creates danger signals, then frames himself as uniquely dangerous yet uniquely devoted.

Bella's Response

Romantic idealization converts the danger into evidence of his special nature. The withholding becomes mystery. The devotion overrides the danger.

Edward's Move

Push-pull: draws close, then withdraws abruptly without naming the reason. Returns when Bella has stopped pursuing.

Bella's Response

Intermittent reinforcement amplifies attachment. The unpredictability of connection makes its presence more valuable, not less.

Edward's Move

Reframes Bella's accurate perceptions as misreadings. "You hit your head." "You don't understand."

Bella's Response

Self-blame absorption: Bella doubts her own perception rather than his account. Her accurate reads get filed as imagination.

Love Model Comparison

Both Edward and Bella express love-priority models — but their underlying structures diverge sharply.

Edward's Love Model

"Love is when everything around you disappears and you only see that one person."

This is an engulfment model — love as the elimination of everything outside the dyad. The partner ceases to exist as a separate entity.

Bella's Love Model

"I would rather die than stay away from you."

This is a devotional model — love as total surrender of self-preservation. The self becomes secondary to the relationship.

Surface compatibility, structural divergence: Both models look love-prioritizing. But Edward's engulfment model requires Bella's absorption into his world, while Bella's devotional model makes her willing to provide exactly that absorption. They appear to want the same thing and are actually building toward very different outcomes.

The Pipeline
HOW THIS ANALYSIS WAS PRODUCED
End-to-End System

Pipeline Architecture

Core Principle: One API call per utterance. The Python script controls the loop. Each LLM call does one small job. This prevents hallucination from processing too much at once.

1

Script Ingestion

Twilight screenplay loaded and segmented by character utterance. Each line tagged with speaker, scene, and timestamp.

2

Per-Line LLM Analysis

Each utterance sent individually to an open-source LLM (NVIDIA GPTOS 120B). Prompt asks: character, trait dimension, signal strength, reasoning.

3

CSV Output

Results saved as structured CSV with columns: character, utterance, claim/tell/perception, trait, signal, reasoning. One row per utterance-trait match.

4

Multi-Platform Scoring

Same analysis run across GPT, Copilot, DeepSeek, and Gemini. Scores averaged to reduce single-model bias.

5

Player Type Matching

Trait score vector compared against all 17 FIA player types using Euclidean distance. Nearest type identified with confidence score.

6

Wiki Publication

Results rendered as this interactive website. Deployed via Vercel. GitHub repository auto-updates on every push.

Who is Jacob?

Jacob Black presents as the safe alternative — warm, grounded, human, and available. He positions himself as everything Edward is not: present rather than distant, warm rather than cold, honest rather than secretive. But the FIA methodology reads function, not affect. The question is not whether Jacob's warmth is real, but what it accomplishes in the relationship.

Jacob's primary pattern is Charming Charlie: high emotional warmth deployed as a relational entry point, combined with persistent pursuit that does not respect Bella's stated boundaries or choices. His loyalty is genuine — but it is conditional on Bella eventually choosing him.

Key Finding: Jacob's warmth is real, but it functions as leverage. When Bella does not reciprocate romantically, Jacob does not accept the boundary — he reframes it as temporary, as Edward's fault, or as Bella not yet knowing what she really wants.

Jacob's Profile

Genuine Strength

Jacob's warmth, humor, and availability are real. He offers Bella something Edward does not: normalcy, grounded connection, and a world outside the supernatural. This is not performance — it is who he is.

The Problem

Jacob's pursuit does not pause when Bella says no. He interprets her friendship as a romantic opening, her uncertainty as encouragement, and her choice of Edward as a mistake he can correct. Respect for her stated preference is absent.

Competitive Framing

Jacob consistently positions Edward as the obstacle between himself and Bella rather than accepting Bella's autonomous choice. This frames Bella as a prize to be won rather than a person making her own decisions.

FIA Type: Charming Charlie

Charming Charlie uses genuine warmth and emotional connection as a relational entry point. The charm is real; the problem is that it comes with an expectation of reciprocation that does not honor the other person's boundaries.
